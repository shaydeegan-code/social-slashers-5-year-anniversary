Our Story

Five Years of Hurling, Craic & Community

The Social Slashers were founded in 2021 with a simple but powerful mission: to reduce social isolation in rural areas amongst the 35+ age group. What began as a weekly puck-around for a handful of ex-players and total beginners has grown into a warm, welcoming social hurling and camogie group open to absolutely everyone — now a registered charity with an elected committee, an official O'Neills team shop, and friends right across the Lecale peninsula.

"A rag-tag rabble of ex-players and brave beginners walking down the boulevard of broken hurls together."
— The Slashers, as featured on OTB / Bord Gáis Energy and TG4

Along the way we've toured the clubs of Lecale, run the Social Dashers 5K for Downpatrick Autism Support, raised funds for mental health and the Kilclief Storm Relief Fund, put a defibrillator pitch-side, and proved — thanks to Gary taking up hurling at 70 — that it's never too late to pick up a hurl.

We Need You

👶 Baby Pictures & Fun Facts Wanted!

One of the big games on the night is "Guess the Slasher" — we'll put baby photos of our players up on the big screen and let the room vote on who's who.

Dig out a baby or childhood photo of your favourite Slasher (or yourself!) and send it in. And if your Slasher has an interesting fact about them — a hidden talent, a claim to fame, a story they'd rather forget — we want that too.

Photos will only be used on the night, and the person's name will be kept secret until the big reveal. Deadline: Friday 11th September.
